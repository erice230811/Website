Create a Layer or Vector Mask


Step 1: Convert it to a regular layer (Layer > New > Layer from Background).
Step 2: You can edit a layer mask to add or subtract from the masked region.
Step 3: The areas you paint in black are hidden, the areas you paint in white are visible, and the areas you paint in shades of gray appear in various levels of transparency.


Step 4: A vector mask creates a sharp-edged shape on a layer and is useful anytime you want to add a design element with clean and defined edges.
Step 5: You can apply one or more layer styles to it, edit them if needed, and instantly have a usable button, panel, or other web-design element.
Step 6: You can change the opacity of a mask to let more or less of the masked content show through.
Step 7: invert the mask, or refine the mask borders, as with a selection area.
